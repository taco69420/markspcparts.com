It was 2021. I was 14, broke, and absolutely addicted to eBay auctions.

I don't even remember what I was originally searching for. But somehow I ended up on a listing for **128 sticks of 1GB DDR2 ECC server RAM** and my brain just... stopped working. Someone had pulled these out of a decommissioned server rack and thrown the whole lot up for auction. No reserve. Starting bid was low.

I said screw it. I bid $20.

I won for **$21.73.**

I was now the owner of 128 gigabytes of DDR2 ECC RAM, 14 years old, sitting in Redlands, with absolutely zero plan.

---

## The Problem With Winning

128 sticks of DDR2 ECC is a lot of RAM when you have no server to put it in.

They showed up in a big anti-static tray, packed in rows, looking like something out of a data center dumpster. Beautiful in a weird way. Completely useless to me at the time.

![128 sticks of 1GB DDR2 ECC server RAM packed in anti-static tray](/images/posts/ddr2-wall-art/ram-sticks-tray.jpg)

So I started giving them away. My school's lab tech — I brought him a stack one day and he genuinely freaked out. Like, full-on couldn't believe it. Those sticks are probably still running in some school computer somewhere in the IE. That felt good.

But I still had a crapton left. Some are under my bed right now. I genuinely don't know what to do with them.

---

## The Wall Art

At some point I started thinking about what to actually *do* with a few of them. Not run them — just display them. These sticks are from a different era of computing. DDR2 ECC, gold contacts, little yellow warning labels still on them. They look cool. They deserve better than a box.

My grandpa had a scrap piece of wood sitting around. We got to talking, figured out a layout, and built a simple wooden frame together. Nothing fancy — just clean cuts, a wire hanger on the back, and 18 sticks of RAM mounted in two columns like a grid.

Hung it on the wall.

![18 sticks of DDR2 ECC RAM mounted in wooden frame, hanging on wall](/images/posts/ddr2-wall-art/ram-wall-v2.jpg)

It looks exactly as unhinged as I hoped. People walk into the room and do a double take every single time. It's genuinely one of my favorite things I own — and it cost me about $3 worth of RAM.

---

## The Rest of Them

I've still got somewhere between 80 and 90 sticks under my bed. Open to ideas. If you want some, reach out — I'll probably just give them away at this point. They're not getting faster sitting in a box.

The $21.73 auction was one of the best financial decisions I've ever made. Not because the RAM was useful. Just because it became *this.*
